A Read on Where SLT Medical Loses Orders Between the Catalog and the Bank.

Ten device lines, a principal who knows every vendor, and a storefront that has been offline since early 2024. The math on that gap is the conversation.

What we already knew before the conversation.

We came in with a few pieces of homework already done. Your catalog (Stemtox-A, Revv Laser Hair Loss, Elevare Red, 3D RF, Hi-EMT Magshape, Faith, the Empire line) is on the archived version of sltmedical.com. The live domain is returning a Wix error page and has been since early 2024. The 866 number still rings. The business is clearly active. The public shopfront is not.

How we would look at it.

Eduba looks at a business like this the way we look at any ops-heavy operator. 60 percent of what eats your week is traditional code and database work: a working site, a product database, an order form, a payment rail. 30 percent is rule-based: device-by-device FDA status, per-clinic financing terms, per-state shipping rules. Ten percent is genuine AI: the assistant that answers a clinic owner at 9 p.m. with the same answer your team would give, and books the demo.

Most distributors in your category pay agencies to rebuild the whole stack as if it were all AI. That is why the projects cost eighty thousand and ship late. We would not do that to you.

A case that maps.

Simple Lyfe Stays runs 46 short-term rental properties with one owner making every meaningful call. We wrote an orchestration layer that turned the owner's head-knowledge into a folder tree an assistant could read. The deliverable was not a new app. It was a shared context the team could act from. You are a ten-SKU version of the same problem.

The method.

The method we use to externalize one person's head into a shared, inspectable context is called Interpretable Context Methodology. The paper is on GitHub and submitted to ACM TiiS: github.com/RinDig/Interpretable-Context-Methodology-ICM-.

Jake has also trained 1,500+ people across enterprise engagements since May 2025, including KPMG UK (one of the Big Four) and the Pacific Life / Colgate-Palmolive cohorts with Correlation One.
